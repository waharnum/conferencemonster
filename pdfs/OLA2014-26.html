WORKING RELATIONSHIPS: 
THE TOOLKIT

Colin Plows, President, 
Friends of the Ottawa Public 
Library Association

Last year our two-part 
workshop discussed the role of 
Friends of the Library, Library 
Trustees, and the Library 
Director. We explored the 
importance of well-defined 
working relationships, so 
that roles and responsibilities 
are well understood. Over 
the past year delegates 
have contributed additional 
feedback, and the end result is 
a toolkit, containing templates, 
policies, memorandums of 
understanding, checklists, and 
resources for you to use in your 
community.

Jami van Haaften, Friends of Canadian

Governance, Succession planning

ARE LIBRARIES PLANNING 
FOR THE FUTURE?

Esrick Quintyn, Principal 
Human Resources Consultant/
Advisor, Quintyn & Associates

Succession Planning is a great 
concern for employers in 
North America as CEOs, and 
senior managers are retiring. 
A huge shortage is ahead as 
the baby-boomer generation 
begins to retire. The impact will 
be huge in Canada for all levels 
of employees. Baby-boomers 
comprise about one third of 
the total Canadian population 
and are slated to reach the 
retirement age. This is a critical 
concern in many organizations 
where departing executives, and 
managers, represent the most 
experienced leaders.

Greg Burns, Port Hope PL Board
