UNITED WE STAND: 
REPORTS FROM ACADEMIC 
EBOOK CONSORTIA

Lou Duggan, Cataloguing & 
Metadata Librarian, Saint Mary’s 
University; Thomas Guignard, 
Ebook Project Manager, OCLS; 
Bill Slauenwhite, Manager, Novanet

Many opportunities and 
challenges face consortia when 
purchasing and managing 
ebooks such as cost savings, 
collection rationalization, 
and central management of 
technical issues. However, an 
access and pricing model that 
stands outside of publisher 
norms is difficult to negotiate. 
We will describe two consortia 
ebook projects: one a new 
proposal at OCLS and the other 
a pilot patron-driven program at 
the Novanet Consortium, which 
includes the Universities and 
Community Colleges in Nova 
Scotia.

Ralph Laird, Niagara College

WHAT’S SO “SPECIAL” 
ABOUT SPECIAL 
COLLECTIONS?

David Sharron, Head of Special 
Collections & Archives, Brock 
University; Rick Stapleton, Archives 
& Research Collections Librarian, 
McMaster University Library

Special collections are often 
touted as what sets one library 
apart from another. While 
most of these collections 
have developed over many 
years, special collections are 
increasingly being asked to 
demonstrate their relevance 
to new areas of teaching and 
research. Hear how McMaster 
is developing a new collections 
policy in consultation with the 
community, and how Brock 
is seizing interdisciplinary by 
collecting in a new area: autism.

Val Lem, Ryerson University

ACADEMIC LIBRARIES

PART I: PROVOSTS & THE 
ACADEMIC LIBRARY – 
PERSPECTIVES & INSIGHTS
Mark Robertson, Associate 
University Librarian, York University

Following interviews in spring 
2013 with nine academic 
provosts of large Canadian 
research-intensive universities, 
the research background 
and overarching themes that 
emerged will be discussed. The 
interviews focused on three 
related questions: 

1.  how do provosts perceive 

academic libraries in 
terms of alignment with 
institutional goals?;

2. how do provosts envision 

the future of their academic 
libraries?; and

3. what do they see as 

measures of success?

PART II: HOW UNIVERSITIES 
WORK: EVERYTHING YOU 
SHOULD KNOW ABOUT 
YOUR FUTURE EMPLOYER
Scott Gillies, Head, Information 
Resources Team

New academic librarians are 
often not fully cognizant of how 
their parent institutions operate. 
We will look at governance and 
funding, budgets, unions and 
faculty associations, hiring 
committees, academic status, 
and more.

Jacqueline Kreller-Vanderkooy, Wilfred 
Laurier University

Legislative Assembly of Ontario

Hansard editors work to create 
the official record the Provincial 
Legislature, and in doing so 
require access to a wide variety 
of resources, and the ability 
to easily share information. 
To meet these needs, we built 
an intranet site using the 
Drupal open-platform content 
management system. Learn 
how we took advantage of 
open-source software and, got 
buy-in from users, proving that 
it is possible to develop a site 
that is user-centered, flexible, 
and that can grow with your 
organization.
