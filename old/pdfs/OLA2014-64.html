  DIGITAL LITERACY & CITIZENSHIP:  

RE-IMAGINING SELF-DIRECTED PROGRAMMING

Melanie Parlette-Stewart, Blended Learning Librarian, Learning 
& Curriculum Support; Lindsey Robinson, Library Associate, 
Learning & Curriculum Support; University of Guelph

The redesign of Self-Directed Programming at the 
University of Guelph Library has led to the creation of 
workshops that target digital-literacy skill development 
and digital citizenship. Through these workshops we 
combine digital-literacy skills and tool utilization to allow 
students to successfully manage and create opportunities 
for themselves as digital citizens. Workshop content is 
created for both face-to-face and online, allowing for a 
blended experience. A sample of workshop topics include: 
infographics, Google Scholar, Zotero, and digital identity 
management.

  FEDERAL LIBRARY CLOSURES OF 2012:  

A RESCUE EFFORT

Colin Harkness, Gifts Co-ordinator; David Sharp, Head of 
Collections, E-Resources & Serials; Carleton University

On March 29, 2012, the federal government tabled a budget 
that set into motion budget cutbacks and even the outright 
closure of many Federal departmental, Crown Corporation, 
Advisory Body, and Non-Profit Organization libraries. 
Over the course of 2012 and early 2013, Carleton University 
Library engaged with 19 libraries in the Ottawa area in an 
effort to rescue print and electronic collections. This poster 
session would like to visually recount the successes, the 
partial successes, as well as the not so happy endings.

 E-LEARNING FOR  

LIBRARY ASSISTANTS & SHELVERS

Ursula Trescases, Co-ordinator of Part-time Staff; Weina Wang, 
Borrowing & Lending Services System Librarian; Ryerson University

This poster illustrates an interactive e-learning call 
number tutorial created for library assistants, student 
assistants, and shelvers. Every year, Ryerson University 
Library employs between 30–50 new student assistants 
and shelvers, in addition to the regular new hirings. 
Since they all work at different schedules and come from 
various backgrounds, the demand to offer a consistent 
training approach and being able to assess the skills staff 
has learned in time was high. This local designed online 
tutorial, sitting in the university’s learning management 
system blackboard, has been extremely popular and 
successful and hundreds of student staff and new 
employees have benefited from it.

  ESL BOOK CLUB

Brenda Vegso, Facilitator, Librarian, Guelph PL

The goal of the Guelph Public Library is to reach out to all 
members in the Guelph community, including newcomers. 
The ESL Book Club was established to bridge a gap 
between newcomers and the library. The program is an 
effective introduction to the library. As facilitator of the 
program, we are in our fourth year and have a standing 
group, with a waiting list. The program has been quoted 
in PRWeb as an effective way to integrate newcomers 
into the community and their new country. The poster 
will share how this program operates and introduce it to 
other libraries as a means of facilitating the integration of 
newcomers into the library community.

  SOMETHING OLD, SOMETHING NEW:  

CREATING A LIBRARIAN MENTORSHIP PROGRAM

Sarah Fedko, Liaison Librarian; Whitney Kemble, Liaison 
Librarian; University of Toronto Scarborough

During the 2013–2014 academic year, librarians at 
University of Toronto Scarborough are piloting a 
mentorship program for liaison librarians. Experienced 
librarians are paired with newer librarians to provide advice 
and support for liaison librarianship. Our poster will include 
an overview of current best practices in mentorship, as 
well as a description of our mentorship program. We will 
also discuss feedback that we have received from program 
participants, and future plans to ensure the effectiveness 
of this program as a professional development opportunity 
for new librarians.

  ON MESSAGE & IN-THE-KNOW

Nancy Collins, Communications & Liaison 
Librarian, University of Waterloo; Margaret Wall, 
Communications Librarian, University of Toronto

All library staff play a role in communicating the library’s 
value. When it comes to communications and marketing, 
how knowledgeable and empowered are your library’s 
staff? Do they have the tools they need to effectively 
and consistently communicate the library’s value? This 
poster will present practical ideas for building capacity in 
communications across your organization, based on guides 
created for colleagues by Communications Librarians at the 
University of Toronto and the University of Waterloo.
