 Advocacy

Ensuring the Future:

 

The library board & 
effective advocacy

Wendy Newman, 
Senior Fellow, Faculty 
of Information, University of Toronto

Advocacy is like a bank account. We have to make 
the deposits before we can make the withdrawals. 
What deposits? Where? Who makes them? 
(Spoiler alert: WE do.) And what are the key 
relationships that make all the difference? Join us 
for this special election-year session on evidence-
based contemporary advocacy that’s rooted in 
the timeless values of our communities and our 
libraries.
Wendy Newman is Senior Fellow at the Faculty 
of Information, University of Toronto. She has an 
extensive background in public, university, and 
educational librarianship as well as public policy 
advocacy.
She is former President of the Canadian Library 
Association and the Canadian Association of Public 
Libraries. She was awarded the Ontario Public 
Library Association Lifetime Achievement Award in 
January 2009, and in 2004 received both the CLA’s 
Outstanding Service to Librarianship Award and 
the Faculty of Information Alumni Jubilee Award. 
She also holds a Queen Elizabeth II Golden Jubilee 
Medal (2003) for community service. 
As a public library director, Wendy has led ground-
breaking partnerships that build community 
capacity and advance economic and social 
strengths. A passionate advocate for libraries and 
librarianship in the public policy arena, she served 
on the National Broadband Task Force, the Blue 
Ribbon Panel on Smart Communities, and the 
Board of Directors of MediaSmarts. 

Kim Vares, 2013 OLBA President

DIGITAL CITATIONS & 
NOTE-TAKING WITH 
NOODLETOOLS

Marilyn Girndt, Library Department 
Head; Mark McLaren, Teacher-
Librarian; Durham DSB

Are your teachers tired of 
marking incorrect and poorly 
formatted Works Cited pages? 
Do your students know how 
to accurately cite information 
in a research paper? Consider 
subscribing to NoodleTools – an 
online citation and note-taking 
software service. Since we have 
started to use this software in 
our school, teachers, students, 
and teacher-librarians are 
amazed at the power and ease 
of creating accurate citations 
in APA, MLA, and other styles. 
Secondary

Ebooks

IMPLEMENTING 
E-READERS & EBOOKS IN 
YOUR LIBRARY & SCHOOL

Cynthia Gozzard, Teacher-
Librarian, Grand Erie DSB

The era of digital readers 
is here. Many already have 
e-readers and/or ebooks within 
your school. Now comes the 
hard part: disseminating all 
the knowledge you have as a 
librarian on how to use them. 
Ebooks are invisible on your 
library shelf and e-readers can 
be tucked away never to be 
used. Find practical examples 
of how to implement e-readers 
and ebooks in your school 
with students ELK through 
grade 8. Help your staff and 
students use digital reading 
on a daily basis for research, 
guided reading, shared reading, 
and independent reading. 
Elementary

Mary Jo Wheeler-Ali, Peel DSB
