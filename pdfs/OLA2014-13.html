Kids Learning Code and MakerKids will be creating a pop-up 
makerspace on the OLA conference exhibition floor! Come 
and see a typical layout, and learn some activities by trying 
them yourself. You’ll get to see toy hacking, Arduino robot 
rovers, Cube 3D Printers from 3D Systems, HTML, MIT’s 
Scratch, and more. The activities are designed to be run in 
your own spaces, and you will be supplied with teaching 
materials to show you how! Check out their websites, 
www.makerkids.ca and www.kidslearningcode.com, and ask 
them about their Making Makers modules which they are 
developing for teachers and librarians to use to teach maker 
activities to kids. Library staff from some of the Ontario 
libraries already involved in the maker culture movement will 
be popping in over the 2-day Super EXPO time to discuss 
and display their maker projects and programs.

Beyond Literacy Radio:  

Exploring a Post-Literate Future

Beyond Literacy Radio is a thought experiment about the 
possibility of a post-literate future conducted by Michael 
Ridley and a class of graduate students at the iSchool, 
University of Toronto. Using podcasts and various social 
media, the project explored post-literacy and engaged 
listeners in a dialogue. At Super Conference, several of the 
students will be interviewing people, and then creating 
podcasts to be uploaded and shared!
