Meet your 2014 OLA President

Anita Brooks Kirkland

B.Mus, B.Ed, MM, MLIS
Ontario Certified Teacher

Anita is an instructional leader for 

school library programs, and has particular expertise in 
instructional strategies for discovery and guided inquiry, 
information literacy and digital fluency, the virtual library 
space and the library as a learning commons. She frequently 
shares her knowledge through conference presentations and 
professional articles. She was a member of the writing team 
for Ontario’s guideline for school library programs, Together 
for Learning, and is currently on the steering committee that 
continues to develop resources for implementation.

Anita has a long history of involvement in OLA and OSLA. 
Looking forward to serving the OLA as President, she 
reflects, “In these exciting yet challenging times, librarians 
are busy transforming our understanding of ourselves, and 
the very role of libraries in developing the communities that 
we serve. The core dispositions for library staff and trustees 
in this context are the abilities to learn, innovate, collaborate, 
and lead in new ways. The Ontario Library Association 
embodies these dispositions as it boldly explores ways to 
support its members and advance the role of libraries.”
