PROGRAMMING  
THE WEB WITH API

s

John Fink, Digital Scholarship 
Librarian, McMaster University; 
Tim Ribaric, Digital Services 
Librarian, Brock University

The API (Application Program 
Interface) is a fundamental 
method of information sharing 
used by the Internet. APIs are 
responsible for creating mash-
ups and getting data into and 
out of different web services. 
For example the newly created 
Digital Public Library of America 
provides access to an API for 
anyone curious with interacting 
with the system. Notorious, 
however, in library systems 
is the lack of APIs offered by 
vendors and the dreaded screen 
scraping techniques that need 
to be used instead. Learn the 
basics of APIs, and why they are 
important in library services.

Kim Pham, University of Toronto

HIDDEN WORLD  
OF LAW LIBRARIES

Karen Sawatzky, Librarian, 
Tapper Cuddy LLP; Brenda Wong, 
Library Technician, McMillan

Law libraries are a unique 
corporate environment. Library 
technicians may be involved 
in building databases, current 
awareness, and training. 
Ongoing communication and 
marketing are important factors 
in a successful law library, 
as well as communicating 
effectively with staff, promoting 
the value of the library service 
to upper management, and 
networking with other law 
libraries. Sharpen your skills 
for your current job or career 
through volunteering, blogging, 
and contract work.

Dana Schwartz, Royal Bank of Canada

The complexities of work and 
the unrelenting pace of change 
suggest that we look beyond the 
classic one-to-one mentoring 
relationship between a senior 
and junior person. Based on
