IMPACTING LIVES IN THE MAASAI MARA

Scott Baker, Executive Director, Free The Children

In 2006 the OLA and the Larry Moore Fund supported 
the building of two libraries in the Maasai Mara of Kenya 
in memory of Elizabeth Hoffman, OLA President 2000. 
Eight years later, speakers from Free The Children, the 
organization which built the libraries, will walk through the 
impact this has had on the communities and share stories of 
students who have benefited from the projects. This session 
will provide insights into the differences in education 
between Ontario schools and those of the Maasai Mara, 
and provide people with a chance to learn more about the 
continued efforts of the OLA to support students in Kenya.
Scott Baker has been integral in shaping the success of Free 
The Children – which started in 1995 as a small group of 12 
year olds – into the world’s leading youth-driven charity. He 
has been with Free The Children since 2004 and has played 
an important leadership role in guiding the growth of the 
organization. In addition to managing many foundational 
school board partnerships and partner relationships, Scott’s 
constant dedication and commitment played a key role in 
the initial bond with Free The Children and youth at home. 
This bond has now grown into a network of more than one 
million youth involved in Free The Children’s programs in 45 
countries.

Peggy Thomas, Toronto PL
