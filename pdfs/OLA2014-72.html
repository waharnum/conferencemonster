to effectively communicating 
value: visceral understanding 
of what your employer values, 
focused measurement of the 
outcomes of your activities as 
they affect those values, and 
persuasive communication 
up the organization. Tips for 
a daily training regimen to be 
ready for the Big Fight: to have 
stakeholders see your library as 
essential and high value-add, 
and therefore to be cherished 
and improved.

BOOKTALKING 3.0: 
ENGAGING & INSPIRING 
READERS ONLINE

Valerie Medzalabanleth, Manager of 
Children’s Services; Melissa Tomecz, 
Reader’s Advisory Services 
Co-ordinator; Côte Saint-Luc PL

Readers’ advisory is a key 
component to library services, 
but with users moving to more 
virtual domains, we must focus 
on virtual booktalking, creating 
an online place for readers’ 
inspiration. While readers’ 
advisory implies a conversation 
between librarian and reader, 
readers’ inspiration doesn’t. 
Using blogs and online book 
clubs, learn to inspire rather 
than advise so patrons continue 
making libraries a part of their 
reading lives.

Pauline Dewan, Wilfrid Laurier

CHANGING LIBRARIES – 
NEW WAYS OF WORKING

Rachel Van Riel, Director, 
Opening the Book

Rachel Van Riel introduces the 
thinking behind new library 
practice in the UK and the 
evidence on user behaviour 
which underlies the new 
approach. She will look at how 
the introduction of self-service 
changes roles and daily tasks 
and how to help staff adjust to 
feeling comfortable working 
away from the help desk. Can 
these changes lead to better 
experiences for patrons and 
more job satisfaction for staff?

The Middle Childhood initiative 
maps out an exciting new 
direction for TPL services to
