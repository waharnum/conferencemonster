BUILDING AN ENGAGING &  
MANAGEABLE LIBRARY 
ADVOCACY STRATEGY

Maureen Barry, CEO; 
Kerry Langford, Vice Chair, Library Board; 
Nicole Paterson, Manager Marketing 
Communications; Burlington PL

Building a library advocacy strategy can 
seem like a daunting task, especially 
if you are starting from scratch. Learn 
about how Burlington Public Library and 
it’s board kick-started library advocacy 
in their community. This session will 
describe the development process, 
setting goals and priorities, volunteer 
recruitment strategies as well as share 
some of the success stories and results 
achieved. As 2014 is an election year in 
Ontario municipalities it will be important for every public 
library to advocate with candidates for municipal office 
to ensure the library interests are understood and will be 
represented over the next term of Council.

Dave Penteliuk, Mississauga PL

Maker culture

MAKER PROJECTS IN ACTION

Jennifer Turliuk, Co-Executive Director (Business) & Chief 
Happiness Officer; Andy Forest, Co-Executive Director 
(Learning), Co-Founder & Chief Instigator, MakerKids

MakerKids is one of the only makerspaces for kids in the 
world and they are here to help libraries do more making 
activities with kids. MakerKids has been preparing detailed 
teaching kits and materials with documentation on how 
to set-up and offer hands-on creative projects in your own 
environments. By OLA Conference time, they plan to have 
prototypes ready for experimentation. They will also have on 
hand Cube 3D printers from 3D Systems. Spend some time 
learning about what MakerKids has to offer your library or 
organization! 

Sarah Niro, Seneca College
