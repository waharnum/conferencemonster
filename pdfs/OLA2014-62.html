  #READY STEADY GO

Karen Brophey, Content & Programs Co-ordinator, Content 
& Access; Theresa Power, Content & Access; CNIB

The CNIB Library undertook our first social media 
campaign with the purpose of raising our online presence 
to the next level. Efforts concentrated on promoting 
an accessible and inclusive TD Summer Reading Club 
(TDSRC) and increasing our follower growth on Twitter 
and Facebook. The campaign ran from April–August 2013 
across Twitter, Fa9cebook, YouTube, Pinterest, the TDSRC 
website, CNIB Library’s website, and email, with efforts 
focused primarily on public library staff.

  PUPPY TALES:  

STORYTIME WITH A TAIL-WAGGING TWIST

Patricia Richard, Manager of Library Services, 
County of Lennox & Addington PL

Lennox & Addington Public Libraries has found success in 
offering programming for young families with the help of a 
certified canine companion. Karma, the story dog, attends 
each weekly session where she provides sound effects for 
stories and woofs her way through each sing-along. Karma 
acts as a novelty draw for reluctant young readers and, 
much to the delight of both parents and tots, allows for 
an interactive and untraditionally boisterous storytime. 
Through sound and play, Karma’s presence has helped the 
library turn reading into an uproarious social event.

  TEACHING VALUES THROUGH STORY: 

SCARBOROUGH STUDENTS EXPLORE ANCIENT 
ORAL EPIC

Brenda Beck, CEO, The Sophia Hilton Foundation;  
Parthi Kandavel, Teacher

In the spring of 2013 a sixth grade homeroom teacher 
showed his Scarborough students 26 freshly animated 
episodes of an ancient Indian oral epic. These videos 
tell a story of confrontation between farmers and native 
hunters as land is cleared and pioneer farms take hold. 
Though unwelcome initially, the farmers gradually become 
assimilated and gain social recognition. The kids were able 
to apply story examples of bullying, self-confidence and 
more to other aspects of their lives. Photos, teacher notes, 
exercises, an iPad story version, videos, and graphic novels 
will be displayed.

  STRENGTHENING A LITERARY  

COMMUNITY WITH A SELF-PUBLISHING LAB

Sue Perry, Self-Publishing Facilitator, Windsor PL

Windsor Public Library has acquired an Espresso Book 
Machine. It has the capability of producing library-quality 
books in 4-7 minutes. While patrons are thrilled to 
experience the tangible realization of their endeavors, there 
has also arisen out of the self-publishing lab a strong sense 
of community. People who have a love of writing gather in 
the lab to talk about the trials and joys of writing, the agony 
of trying to get published, to share marketing ideas, and 
generally lend guidance and support to each other in their 
literary journey.

  L’IL SCIENCE AT THE BELLEVILLE PUBLIC LIBRARY

Suzanne Humphreys, Children’s Librarian, Belleville PL

Budding Einsteins are flocking to the Belleville Public 
Library on Saturday mornings for some preschool science 
fun. Traditionally the public library has done much to 
facilitate early-literacy development. However, the evolving 
public library can do even more to engage young families 
and encourage them to access our programs and materials. 
At the Belleville Public Library we host a program that 
promotes our marvellous non-fiction collection and 
showcases our highly engaging databases while offering 
hands-on experiments which wow the kids and support 
caregivers in an area in which they might not be the 
most comfortable. Let’s encourage children while they 
hypothesize, experiment, and draw conclusions as the 
natural scientists they are and let’s do it at the library!
