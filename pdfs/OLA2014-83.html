Election Year Boot Camp

Kim Vares, OLBA President

Municipal elections will be held on October 27, 2014. In 
the months leading up to the election, public library boards 
engage in reviewing progress, highlighting strengths, 
ensuring strong candidates are recruited, and smoothing 
the transition from current to new board. This Boot Camp 
has been developed to seize the opportunity to build strong 
library boards.

CEO–BOARD CHAIR RELATIONSHIP BUILDING

Susanna Hubbard Krimmer, CEO; 
Josh Morgan, Board Chair; London PL

Josh Morgan and Susanna Hubbard Krimmer have 
developed their CEO–Board Chair relationship over the past 
five years and have worked together in that capacity for the 
past three years. During that time they have led the London 
Public Library through multiple contentious issues (Internet 
filtering, branch closure discussions, budget pressures, 
etc.) Hear about what worked and what didn’t and how they 
fostered a collaborative, productive, and highly-effective 
working relationship.

BUILDING BETTER BOARDS: IT ALL BEGINS 
WITH RECRUITMENT & SELECTION

Matt Brown, Ward 7, City Council, London;  
Josh Morgan, Board Chair, London PL Board;  
Facilitator: Gina La Force, Principal, La Force & Associates

The session will be a dialogue between a library board chair 
and local councillor on board recruitment and selection. 
Issues include: when recruitment begins; the role of 
Councillor Board members; whether boards should become 
involved in board selection; and, how advocacy can be most 
effective from a councillor’s point of view.

SMOOTH THE TRANSITION:  
YOUR BOARD’S LEGACY DOCUMENT

Margaret Andrewes & Randee Loucks, 
Consultants, Building Strong Boards

What’s involved in transitioning from one board to the next? 
Explore your board’s opportunity to grow sound public 
library leadership and governance in your community. 
Sharing your board’s successes and collective wisdom 
through a “legacy letter”, addressed to the new board, is a 
vital responsibility.

Kim Vares, OLBA President & Frances Ryan, Sault Ste. Marie PL Board
