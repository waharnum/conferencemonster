PARTNERING FOR 
DIGITIZATION & ACCESS

Eva Jurczyk, Member Services 
& Licensing Officer, Canadian 
Research Knowledge Network; 
William Wueppelmann, Manager, 
Information Services, Canadiana.org

Learn about a unique 
partnership between LAC, 
Canadiana.org, and a network 
of Canadian universities that 
is undertaking the large-scale 
digitization of primary source 
Canadian heritage material 
to make this material broadly 
accessible. The Heritage 
Collection drew start-up 
funding from Canadian 
university libraries to support 
material digitization, and 
employed a combination of 
fee-for-service, crowd-sourcing, 
and technological solutions to 
fund metadata creation and text 
transcription.

IMPROVING MATERIALS 
HANDLING: ERGONOMIC 
ELASTICS – WHO KNEW?

Karen Anderson, Director of Public 
Service; Simona Dinu, Branch 
Manager; Karen Milligan-Thurston, 
Branch Manager; Karen Peter, 
Branch Manager; Hamilton PL

Materials handling is critical 
to libraries. In 2013, Hamilton 
Public Library examined how we 
were handling (or mishandling) 
material and looked for ways to 
reduce the potential for injury, 
work smarter, and create a set 
of scalable best practices for 
our libraries of all sizes. Armed 
with valuable advice from an 
ergonomist, we are modifying 
our work flows and have some 
“lessons learned” to share.
