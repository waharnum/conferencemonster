Accessibility

eLibrary 
Accessibility 3.0: 

Where are we today?

Jennison Asuncion, 
Co-Director, Adaptech 
Research Network

The Integrated Standard of the Accessibility for 
Ontarians with Disabilities Act – specifically the 2014 
directive around websites needing to meet the Web 
Content Accessibility Guidelines at the A Level for 
new content – has certainly brought renewed focus 
to and interest in digital accessibility.
Learn the most current digital accessibility best 
practices and demonstrate some of the latest 
specialized technologies out there today, which 
together, can be used to provide access to an 
evolving array of digital material and services that 
more and more libraries are making available. By 
implementing these best practices and making 
specialized technology available, libraries can 
increase the level of inclusivity to eCollections and 
eLibrary services for patrons with disabilities.
A recipient of a Queen Elizabeth II Diamond Jubilee 
Medal, Jennison has been conducting research 
into the use and accessibility of information and 
communication technologies by postsecondary 
students with disabilities in Canada since 1999. 
He combines this academic pursuit with over 10 
years of hands-on digital accessibility experience 
working in the Canadian financial services industry. 
A respected leader in the digital accessibility space, 
Jennison dedicates time to making the subject of 
digital accessibility, accessible to the mainstream 
tech community. This includes co-founding Global 
Accessibility Awareness Day in 2012, a day (May 9) 
for people who build, shape, fund, and influence 
technology and its use to become better aware of 
and experience digital accessibility issues faced by 
people with disabilities/impairments.

COACHES’ CORNER

Margaret Macmillan and Julie Mandal, Brampton PL, 
return this year to offer “a taste of coaching” on a 
pre-scheduled basis. Registration will occur early in 
January 2014 through the OLA listservs for 20-minute 
private chats with one of the professional coaches. No 
matter where you are in your career, you are welcome 
to register for your complimentary coaching session.

MEET EMPLOYERS & CONSULTANTS

Library personnel and consultants from various 
sectors will set up table-displays and be present to 
mentor job-seekers. Informal conversations are often 
the triggers that prompt students, new grads, and 
newly employed individuals to rethink directions and 
look to the future with optimism. For example:

• what does the (public, university, college) Library 

System look for in hiring?

• what advice can you give for someone who wants 

to change library sectors?

• how do school libraries staff, and how should 

someone prepare?

• what advice do you have for someone who wants 

to move into management?

• what is the best “dress” for today’s interview? or 

today’s workplace?

• what career advancement tips can you offer?
• now that I have my post-grad degree, what PD 

should I look for?
