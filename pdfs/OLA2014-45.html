Authors, Impact of libraries

Libraries  
Change Lives

Richard Wagamese, Native 
Author & Storyteller

“All that we are is story. From 
the moment we are born to the 
time we continue on our spirit 
journey, we are involved in the creation of the story 
of our time here. It is what we arrive with. It is all we 
leave behind. We are not the things we accumulate. 
We are not the things we deem important. We are 
story. All of us. What comes to matter then is the 
creation of the best possible story while we’re here; 
you, me, us, together. When we can do that and we 
take the time to share those stories with each other, 
we get bigger inside, we see each other, we recognize 
our kinship – we change the world, one story at a 
time…”
Richard Wagamese has a story for everyone 
associated with libraries. He describes it as a 
“powerful” story. It’s also a personal one. It’s about 
how libraries change lives.
Richard Wagamese is one of Canada’s foremost 
Native authors and storytellers. He is the author 
of 12 titles with a new novel, Medicine Walk, 
coming in spring 2014. Richard is the recipient of 
many honours including, most recently, the 2012 
National Aboriginal Achievement Award for Media 
& Communications, the 2013 Molson Prize in the 
Arts, and the choice of his novel, Indian Horse, as 
the 2013–2014 First Nation Communities Read 10th 
anniversary title selection.
