Entrepreneurship

STARTUP LIBRARY 101

MJ D’Elia, Head (Acting), Learning 
& Curriculum Support, University 
of Guelph; Helen Kula, Institute for 
Management & Innovation (IMI), 
University of Toronto Mississauga

In April 2012, Brian Mathews asserted 
in his white paper that libraries need 
to “Think Like a Startup.” But how 
do startups think? If we are going to 
emulate startup culture, then we have 
some learning to do. We will tackle the 
build-measure-learn cycle, validated 
learning, iterative design, continuous improvement, and 
other components of lean thinking. We’ll underscore the 
importance of hands-on development, prototyping, and 
hypothesis testing. Come join the conversation and help 
make entrepreneurial thinking a habitual part of our practice 
and profession.

Amy Hoffmann, Ottawa PL
