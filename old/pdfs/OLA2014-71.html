PARENTS IN THE LIBRARY: 
SHARING STORIES, 
MAKING A DIFFERENCE

Jennifer Carey, Teacher-Librarian; 
Lynne Martens, Community 
Support Worker; Toronto DSB

Developed by the Teacher-
Librarian and a “Model Schools 
for Inner Cities” staff to engage 
parents in a downtown Toronto 
school, the library has hosted 
regular parent gatherings 
to address the interests and 
needs of a diverse newcomer 
population. This year our 
focus has been on writing and 
sharing personal stories in both 
English and home languages. 
Through the process, the 
school community has been 
enriched by the parent voice. 
We will outline the program, 
the data collected, and the 
continuing positive impact in 
the community. Elementary

LIBRARY BOARD 
RECRUITMENT

Jane Hilton, Board Member, Whitby 
PL; Josh Morgan, Board Member, 
London PL; Dave Phillips, Board 
Member, Uxbridge PL; Kim Vares, 
Board Member, Dryden PL

Library board members are 
appointed by their municipal 
councils and with elections 
scheduled in 2014, this 
session will examine the 
different approaches to board 
recruitment. A panel will 
discuss guidelines, tips, tools, 
and personal perspectives on 
recruitment strategies that 
have proven effective. Whether 
or not you presently have a 
succession plan, the board has 
a responsibility in recruiting 
board members to ensure the 
future of a quality public library 
service for the community.

Lynn Humfress-Trute, Middlesex 
County PL Board

MANAGING YOUR 
CEO’S PERFORMANCE

Daryl Novak, Director of 
Operations, SOLS

Geared towards public library 
board members, we will address 
roles and responsibilities for 
CEO performance. We will 
discuss the extent to which 
a CEO’s performance can be 
distinguished from the library’s, 
and the different types of 
support CEOs need depending 
on where they are in their 
careers. For the most part, we 
will concentrate on models 
of performance planning 
and evaluation, and discuss 
strategies for dealing with 
problem performance.

Jamie Morris, City of Kawartha Lakes 
PL Board

ADAPTIVE TECHNOLOGY 
LAB USE: A CROSS-
INSTITUTIONAL UX STUDY

Michele Chittenden, Co-ordinator, 
Library Services for Students with 
Disabilities, Queen’s University; 
Athol Gow, Library Accessibility 
Services, University of Guelph; 
Sandra Halliday, Health Sciences 
Librarian, Queen’s University

The Co-ordinators for Library 
Services for Students with 
Disabilities from Queen’s 
University Library and Guelph 
University Library Accessibility 
Services recently noticed 
shifts in student usage of their 
respective adaptive technology 
(AT) labs. The basic model for 
many AT labs was created over a 
decade ago. Are we still meeting 
the needs of the students? What 
adaptive technologies provided 
by the library are students 
using? Have mobile devices 
replaced desktops? How do 
students use the space? What 
would the ideal adaptive 
technology lab look like?

Rebecca Jones, Dysart & Jones 
Associates
