ASSEMBLE YOUR EBOOK TOOLKIT:   
CREATING THE RIGHT PROGRAM FOR YOUR LIBRARY
Whether you are a librarian in a University, CEGEP or Public 
Library, you already have a large collection or are still trying to 
decide where to begin, there are many ways to acquire ebooks and 
build a program that is right for your library and the end users 
you serve. During this session, you will learn more about the 
variety of “tools” used to acquire ebooks – from access models 
like discounted subscription collections and demand-driven 
acquisition with short-term loans to individual purchases or pre-
selected perpetual packs. Using the unparalleled ingenuity and 
experience of the ebrary and EBL businesses, you will come away 
with a better sense of how to fit the pieces together to design the 
right ebook toolkit for your library!

Pop-Up Makerspace@OLA

Kids Learning Code and MakerKids will be creating a pop-up 
makerspace on the EXPO floor! Come and see a typical layout, 
and learn some activities by trying them yourself. You’ll get to 
see toy hacking, Arduino robot rovers, Cube 3D Printers from 
3D Systems, HTML, MIT’s Scratch, and more. The activities are 
designed to be run in your own spaces, and you will be supplied 
with teaching materials to show you how! Check out their websites, 
www.makerkids.ca and www.kidslearningcode.com, and ask them 
about their Making Makers modules which they are developing 
for teachers and librarians to use to teach maker activities to kids. 
Library staff from some of the Ontario libraries already involved 
in the maker culture movement will be popping in to discuss and 
display their maker projects and programs.
