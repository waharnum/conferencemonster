GOVERNANCE & THE 21

st

 

CENTURY: BEST PRACTICES 
FOR YOUR BOARD

Patrick Gauch, Trustee, Ottawa PL

One of the challenges facing 
non-profit organizations is that 
there isn’t one prescriptive 
or ideal model of non-profit 
governance that can be applied 
or used by library boards. 
Review the current models: 
the Policy Governance, 
the Entrepreneurial, the 
Constituency, and the 
Municipal. Identify the 
characteristics of each model 
and learn the positive and 
negative features of each. 
Discuss best practices and 
learnings for the evolution of 
corporate governance.

Claudette Richardson, Kingston 
Frontenac PL Board

FREEDOM & INNOVATION 
IN LIBRARY CATALOGUES: 
ACCESSING OUR 
COLLECTIONS IN 
2014 & BEYOND

Jocelyne Andrews, Head of 
Systems & Technical Services, 
Westmount PL; MJ Suhonos, 
Digital Technologies Development 
Librarian, Ryerson University

For too long the catalogue 
has been an extension of 
proprietary systems, offering 
little opportunity to influence 
the functionality and usability 
of this mission-critical tool. 
While user expectations and 
our competition have changed 
radically, catalogues have 
not. We will look at some 
current best-in-class catalogue 
examples, and consider the 
future of the catalogue, looking 
at how we can embrace next-
generation trends like linked 
data and the semantic web.

John Dunn, St Catharines PL
