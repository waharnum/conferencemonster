OLA SUPER CONFERENCE 2014

Salute to the Student Volunteers!

Attending Super Conference is an awesome way to become 
more familiar with the library profession! The Ontario Library 
Association believes this, and wants to include as many 
full-time students in Ontario’s library programs as possible.  
Each year the Association offers a full complimentary 
conference in return for a half-day volunteer shift.

Over 200 students will be involved in Super Conference 
2014! They are enrolled in one of the following library 
programs:

•  Algonquin College
•  Durham College
•  McGill University
•  Mohawk College
•  San José State University
•  Seneca College
•  University of Ottawa
•  University of Toronto
•  University of Western Ontario

Our student volunteers are at the conference to answer 
questions, direct you to sessions, help our speakers and 
convenors, and much more. We also need our conference 
delegates to welcome our volunteers – they are the future of 
our profession! When you spot a volunteer in their blue vest, 
ask them about their career plans, their interests, and share 

your stories of the profession. In our annual post conference 
volunteer survey, the top four reasons to volunteer at Super 
Conference are: attending a complimentary conference, 
building the résumé, networking with peers, and networking 
with professionals.

Last year 205 student volunteers contributed 811 volunteer 
hours over the course of five days and almost all of them 
took advantage of the free conference offer!

The OLA Staff and the Conference Planning Team all thank 
you!
