Community-led libraries,

Towards 
Community-Led

John Pateman, CEO, 
Thunder Bay PL

John Pateman has been speaking on the importance 
of fostering relationships with customers and 
the potential for a community-led service model 
in public libraries since 1999. He advocates a 
shift in our thinking, moving from outreach to 
community development, from customer service 
to relationships, from outputs to outcomes, and a 
great deal more. With public libraries facing budget 
cuts, he offers an alternative way forward based on a 
community-led approach to developing needs-based 
library services.
John Pateman has worked in UK public libraries 
for over 30 years at all levels. He was a member 
of the research team which produced the ground-
breaking report Open to All? The Public Library and 
Social Exclusion. John has written a wide range of 
articles and books on public libraries and social 
class, social exclusion, and internationalism. He is 
the co-founder of Information for Social Change and 
has worked closely with members of the Working 
Together Project in Canada. Pateman’s educational 
background includes a BA (Honours) degree in 
History and Politics, a diploma in Library and 
Information Science, a Masters degree in Business 
Administration and designation as Fellow of the 
Chartered Institute of Library and Information 
Professionals. 
He is currently Chief Executive Officer for Thunder 
Bay Public Library. His recent book, co-authored with 
Ken Williment, Developing Community-Led Public 
Libraries: Evidence from the UK and Canada was 
released in February 2013.

Joanna Aegard, Thunder Bay PL
