Future trends, Maker culture

MakerSpaces:  
Retinkering school 
libraries

Bill Derry, Assistant 
Director for Innovation 
& User Experience,The 
Westport Library
Andy Forest, Co-Executive 
Director (Learning), Co-
Founder and Chief Instigator, 
MakerKids Toronto

As culture and technology rapidly changes, schools 
and libraries are evolving to meet new needs. 
What are some of the essential concepts involved 
in establishing a MakerSpace in a school and how 
does a teacher-librarian demonstrate leadership? 
Learn the philosophy behind maker culture, and 
consider how students can be inspired by it!
Bill Derry is the Assistant Director for Innovation 
and User Experience at The Westport Library 
in Westport, Connecticut, the co-chair of the 
Westport Mini Maker Faire, and one of the leaders 
of the creation of the Library MakerSpace. Prior to 
joining the Library in 2011, Bill worked in education 
for over 30 years as an elementary classroom 
teacher, drama-in-education teacher, library media 
specialist, supervisor of library media K–12, and 
information and technology literacy co-ordinator. 
MakerKids is a non-profit organization based 
in Toronto that empowers kids to think, design, 
experiment, and then create using a range of tools 
and materials. As the co-founder of one of the first 
makerspaces for kids, Andy Forest is an often-
requested speaker – most recently at Mozfest in 
London, England; the Toronto Mini Maker Faire; 
and the Creative Making in Libraries and Museums 
event at I-School. He is a lifelong tinkerer and head 
of learning for MakerKids. In his own words, “Kids 
are brimming with creative ideas, I have so much 
fun helping them make them happen! I strongly 
believe that the sooner we can convince people to 
be creators, not just consumers, the better off the 
world will be.” Both

Isabelle Hobbs, 2013 President, OSLA

The Guidelines assist public 
libraries to develop and 
maintain consistently strong 
service. They provide boards, 
managers, staff, and local 
governments with up-to-
date guidelines on policies, 
resources, and services. The 
OPLG have four goals, the 
last of which is “to recognize, 
through an accreditation 
process, the achievement 
of those public libraries 
which meet the Guidelines’ 
requirements.” Including AODA 
compliance.

THROUGH THE LENS

Steve Marks, Digital Preservation

Nick Ruest, Digital Assets

More and more, some of the 
most important assets libraries 
have are digital. We need to 
curate these objects with 
the same rigor and expertise 
that we apply to our physical 
collections. Follow a digital 
object through the entire 
digital curation lifecycle, with 
an eye toward identifying the 
important duties of libraries 
in maintaining their digital 
collections, and focusing on 
tools you can use now.
