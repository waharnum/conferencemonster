The Federation of Ontario 
Public Libraries (FOPL) and 
OLA are partnering on a 
major engagement campaign 
focused on the value, impact, 
and positioning of public 
libraries in Ontario’s society, 
culture, and economy. Come 
hear about these exciting plans 
and be ready to engage. Not 
inconsequentially, there will be 
tips and tricks for educating,

the role of public libraries to 
those engaged in the 2014 
municipal and provincial 
elections. Highlighting training

points, visuals, and stories that 
empower all librarians, staff, 
trustees, cardholders, and 
library friends to speak about 
the power of public libraries to 
transform communities.

hospital, Sunnybrook, officially 
opened in 1948 to serve WWI 
and WWII veterans. Librarians

with resources. The Royal 
Canadian Military Institute

largest private library of its kind 
open to the public in Canada.

USING CAMPUS IN YOUR 
LIBRARY OR CLASSROOM

Renée Lottridge, Education 
Specialist; Claudia Sicondolfo, 
Team Leader/Education Specialist; 
National Film Board of Canada

This session will demonstrate 
CAMPUS – the NFB’s online 
streaming room – and provide 
participants with effective 
strategies for integrating 
films, interactive projects, and 
educational materials into 
their libraries and classrooms. 
CAMPUS features 3,000+ 
on-demand films, interactive 
productions, MARC Records, 
pedagogical evaluations, and 
a wide variety of features. The 
session informs on the site’s 
tools and teaches participants 
to use these tools to curate 
thematic and relevant viewing 
lists for their clients.

Cynthia Mckeich, Seneca College

BRINGING MULTICULTURAL 
HISTORY ONLINE: 
DIGITIZING ONTARIO’S 
DIVERSE COMMUNITY 
MEMORY

Catherine Leekam, Program 
Manager, Multicultural History 
Society of Ontario; Jess Posgate, 
Community Outreach Co-
ordinator, OurDigitalWorld

The Multicultural History 
Society of Ontario has 
recently undertaken two 
major digitization projects 
including oral histories 
and multilingual historic 
newspapers in partnership 
with OurDigitalWorld. Explore 
the factors that inspired the 
creation of a digital archive 
and the challenges and new 
opportunities that have 
emerged. Topics will cover 
the implementation of the 
digitization process, cultivating 
partnerships, funding and 
volunteerism, aspects of the 
day-to-day management of the 
Society’s digital collections, and 
discussion of the project’s wider 
implications for the MHSO’s 
overall data management 
and program management 
strategies.
